Understanding Unix/Linux Programming
Unix ClipArt
Every chapter includes the original images used to make the illustrations in the text. The pictures do not have any labels or arrows; those were added later. You have to read the book to know what the pictures represent.

There are two sizes of each image: thumbnail and regular.

What Are They for?
Make Slides

You can use these pictures to make slide presentations for classes. By adding labels, text, and arrows, you can create your own versions of the illustrations in the book. If you use these in your own presentations, your students can recognize the images from the reading, but the emphasis and explanations will match your own way of thinking about the ideas.

Study Guide/Outline

These pictures can also be used as a study guide for students. You can print out each page of thumbnail images as a visual summary of the chapter. Students can review the chapter by looking at the pictures, explaining what each component of each image represents, and adding labels. The way biology students label parts of a cell or the organs of a dissected frog.

Clip Art

You can put them on your course web site as decoration and explanation.
